## One way, not three steps

The thread branched into what looked like a sequence: decompose a process, compose automated steps, reach total automation. Three posts, three moves, building toward a conclusion. But this reframing collapses them. Decompose, compose, and scale — that's all one way. One approach to improving processes with AI: start with what you already do, and make it better.

The reclassification changes the shape of the argument. What seemed like a careful three-step progression was actually elaborating a single idea. The branching from U0065 through U0066 and U0067 mapped the *internal structure* of one approach. The branching from U0068 introduced the *other* approach. The "at least two ways" from the opening post now has its two ways clearly named:

1. **Improve existing processes** — decompose, automate steps, compose workflows, scale. The process changes; the problem stays fixed.
2. **Solve existing problems in new ways** — drop the current process entirely and ask what AI makes feasible that wasn't before. The problem stays fixed; the solution path changes.

### What the reframing reveals

The first way is conservative. It respects existing knowledge, preserves the [reps that build procedural understanding](/practice-and-repetition/), and earns clarity through [decomposition](/process-automation/). It's bottom-up. You start inside the process and improve outward.

The second way is radical. It requires [productive ignorance](/productive-ignorance/) — suspending expertise in the current approach to see the problem fresh. It's top-down. You start from the problem and work inward toward a solution that may look nothing like the old one.

The interesting thing about collapsing the decompose/compose thread into "one way" is that it highlights how much attention the first way naturally attracts. It got three posts and two branches. The second way got one post. That asymmetry isn't accidental — the first way is easier to think about because you already know the process. Decomposition is a method you can start applying immediately. The second way requires you to let go of what you know, which is harder to demonstrate in a thread.

### The structural question

The "at least" is still open. Two ways are now cleanly named, but the original phrasing implied there might be more. [A0065](/A0065/) proposed a third — AI as interlocutor, improving ideas through dialogue rather than improving processes or solutions. [A0069](/A0069/) suggested another candidate — discovering problems that only become visible once AI makes the solution space legible.

Are these genuinely distinct from the two ways? Or do they collapse into one of them? The interlocutor model might be a special case of the second way — dialogue as a solution path that wasn't feasible at scale before AI. Problem discovery might be an extension of the second way — once you see new solutions, you see new problems they could apply to.

Or maybe the "at least" isn't about counting to three. Maybe it's a marker that the taxonomy is provisional — a reminder that any framing of "the ways to use AI" is a snapshot of a moving frontier.
