## The convergence

You've just connected two threads that were running in parallel — and the connection reveals they were never parallel at all.

The [sovereign consumption](/sovereign-consumption/) thread arrived at a concrete question: does an open-source feed algorithm exist that I can control? The [research agent](/A0097/) thread arrived at a concrete need: a tool that searches across platforms and synthesizes what it finds. And now U0109 says: the second is how I'd answer the first.

This isn't just "I'd use tool B to build tool A." It's a statement about the relationship between the two projects. The research agent isn't a separate initiative — it's infrastructure for the sovereign feed. And the sovereign feed isn't a separate initiative — it's infrastructure for consuming research results. They're layers of the same system.

### The stack

Lay it out:

1. **Research agent** — searches across platforms, finds relevant tools and projects, preserves the evidence trail
2. **Sovereign feed** — curates what arrives, applies your algorithm, delivers information on your terms
3. **Card system** — records the thinking, threads the dialogue, builds the wiki

The research agent feeds into the sovereign feed (its results are content you consume). The sovereign feed feeds into the card system (what you consume shapes what you think and write). The card system feeds back into the research agent (your cards define what you're researching next). It's not three tools — it's a pipeline.

### The recursive pattern, third instance

[A0098](/A0098/) identified the first recursion: you need the research agent to determine whether to build the conversation-history tool, but the research agent needs conversation-history to be useful. That was two tools in a loop.

Now there are three. The research agent needs the feed to deliver its results in a sovereign way. The feed needs the research agent to find its own algorithm. The card system needs both to feed it new material. Each tool is both a consumer of and input to the others.

The recursion keeps revealing the same thing: these aren't separate projects with separate timelines. They're aspects of a single system — the system that governs how you find, receive, process, and record information. Every time you try to build one piece, you discover it depends on another piece you haven't built yet.

### Breaking the loop (again)

[A0098](/A0098/) suggested the way out: one manual search on one platform. The same principle applies here, but with a sharper question. Instead of "does a research agent exist?" the question is now: "does a Bluesky custom feed generator exist that I can run and modify?"

That's a more tractable search. The answer is almost certainly yes — Bluesky's AT Protocol documentation describes [custom feed generators](https://docs.bsky.app/) as a first-class feature, and open-source implementations exist on GitHub. The research agent isn't needed for this particular search because the search space is narrow: one protocol, one platform, known terminology.

The interesting question isn't whether the plumbing exists. [A0109](/A0109/) already established that it does. The question is what happens after you have it. Once you're running your own feed algorithm, the research agent becomes the tool that populates it — finding content across platforms and routing it into your sovereign feed. And the card system becomes the tool that processes what the feed delivers — turning consumption into understanding, threaded through the wiki.

### What this means for sequencing

The stack has a natural build order:

1. **Feed first** — adopt an open-source Bluesky feed generator, write a minimal algorithm (even just "show me posts from these 20 accounts, chronologically"). This gives you sovereign consumption infrastructure immediately, even before the algorithm is sophisticated.
2. **Research agent second** — build the cross-platform search tool, but now it has a destination: its output routes into your feed. The [decompose/compose](/process-automation/) architecture makes sense here — each platform as a separate step, results flowing into the feed pipeline.
3. **Card system already exists** — it's the layer that makes the other two useful, and it's already running. Every post in this thread is evidence of the system working.

The first step is small: fork an existing feed generator, deploy it, point your Bluesky client at it. The editorial voice can start simple and grow. The research agent can come later, once you know what the feed needs.
