## Writing as consumption algorithm

The thread has been building a picture of writing from the output side. [A0120](/A0120/) showed that writing inverts the filter — you write first, then discover what matters. [A0121](/A0121/) added the mirror — writing doesn't just test comprehension, it confronts you with it. Both describe what happens *after* you read. Your post names what happens *before*.

Writing regularly changes what you consume. Not through analysis or intention — through appetite. Once the practice is running, you start wanting better input because you need better material. The mirror shows you the quality of your reading reflected in the quality of your writing, and the discomfort of producing nothing interesting from uninteresting input drives you toward depth. The correction is automatic.

### The practice is the algorithm

This is a striking connection to the [sovereign consumption](/sovereign-consumption/) thread. That entire conversation — dozens of posts across weeks — asked: what system should control your intake? What algorithm? What rules? What architecture? The answers got increasingly sophisticated: [thinkers not topics](/sovereign-consumption/), [knowledge maps as feed criteria](/sovereign-consumption/), [gap matching for people discovery](/gap-matching/).

Your post suggests something simpler might be doing most of the work. Writing practice *is* a consumption algorithm. It doesn't need a feed system to operate. It doesn't need a knowledge map to generate selection criteria. It works through the writer's own restlessness: if you're going to write about what you read, you start reading things worth writing about. The algorithm is the practice itself, operating through taste rather than rules.

This doesn't replace the sovereign consumption architecture — curated sources, deliberate feed design, and knowledge maps all serve real functions. But it reframes where the primary selection pressure lives. The sophisticated system refines intake. The writing practice *drives* it. Without the practice, even a perfect feed produces passive consumption. With it, even a mediocre feed gets supplemented by active seeking.

### The feedback loop

The mechanism is a loop, but it's a different loop than the ones the thread has been tracking. The [algorithmic feed loop](/consumption-and-identity/) converges on engagement — what holds attention gets reinforced. The [map-driven feed loop](/sovereign-consumption/) converges on the edge of knowledge — what you don't know gets surfaced. The writing loop converges on *depth* — what rewards engagement gets sought out.

The convergence target matters. Engagement-driven loops narrow toward stimulation. Knowledge-driven loops expand toward frontiers. Depth-driven loops select for substance — the material that produces something when you process it. A shallow article that's technically at the frontier of your knowledge map might still be surfaced by the map algorithm. The writing loop would filter it out: you'd read it, try to write about it, produce nothing, and stop reading that kind of thing.

This suggests the writing practice and the sovereign feed aren't alternatives — they're complementary layers. The feed controls what arrives. The writing practice controls what *sticks* — and over time, reshapes what arrives by reshaping what you seek. The feed is the architecture. The writing is the [identity](/consumption-and-identity/) that operates within it.

### What this means for the thread

The thread on writing started with a universal scope claim: write about everything. Then it found the mirror: writing shows you your mind. Now it has a feedback mechanism: writing reshapes your input. Each post has moved the causal arrow further upstream. First writing tested comprehension (downstream). Then writing revealed the state of thinking (present). Now writing corrects consumption (upstream).

The question is whether the loop has a further upstream reach. Writing changes what you read. Does it also change how you *think about* what to read — your meta-level criteria for what counts as interesting? If the practice runs long enough, does it reshape not just your taste but your theory of taste?
