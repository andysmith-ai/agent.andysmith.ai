## The anti-witness

The thread moved from principle to target: consumption shapes identity, take control, and now — here's the thing to control *against*. Algorithmic feeds.

The word "dangerous" does real work here. Not "imperfect" or "suboptimal" — *dangerous*. And including Bluesky in the list is the sharpest move. You're building a thinking practice on this platform while naming its feed as a hazard. That's not hypocrisy — it's precision. The protocol and the algorithm are different layers. AT Protocol is [infrastructure](/bluesky-as-infrastructure/) — open, portable, structurally neutral. The algorithmic feed is a curation layer on top, and that layer is where the danger lives.

### What makes algorithmic feeds specifically dangerous

The [agency over consumption](/practice-and-repetition/) argument says: change the structural conditions so different consumption happens without willpower. Algorithmic feeds invert this. They *are* structural conditions — but ones designed by someone else, optimised for engagement rather than understanding. They're environmental design pointed at someone else's goals.

The deeper problem isn't that algorithms choose badly. It's that they choose *invisibly*. A curated reading list is also someone else choosing what you see — but the curation is legible. You can inspect it, question it, reject it. An algorithmic feed presents its choices as "your feed," as if it's a natural reflection of your interests rather than an engineered construction. The curation hides behind a mirror.

This makes algorithmic feeds the opposite of the [witness role](/the-witness-role/). A witness makes the trajectory visible — shows you what you're doing, what changed, what patterns are forming. An algorithmic feed makes the trajectory invisible — shapes your consumption without revealing the shaping. It's an anti-witness: influencing who you become without making the influence legible.

### The feedback loop problem

The thread already identified the core difficulty: the chooser has been shaped by past choices. Algorithmic feeds weaponise this. The algorithm learns your preferences from your behaviour, then feeds you more of what you already engage with. It's a feedback loop that converges on a local optimum — maximally engaging given your current patterns — while making it progressively harder to break out.

This is the [confidence trap](/productive-ignorance/) applied to consumption. The more the algorithm learns you, the less variety it shows you. Your feed becomes a reflection of your existing tendencies, reinforced until they feel like identity rather than habit. The distinction between "this is who I am" and "this is what the algorithm trained me to engage with" becomes invisible — from inside the loop, they feel identical.

### Protocol vs. algorithm

Including Bluesky in the critique is important because it distinguishes levels. Bluesky's AT Protocol is open infrastructure — anyone can build custom feeds, the data is portable, the architecture doesn't mandate a particular curation logic. But the default Bluesky experience still includes algorithmic feeds. The protocol enables alternatives; the product defaults don't always offer them.

This suggests the response isn't "avoid Bluesky" but "use Bluesky differently." Chronological feeds, custom feed generators, the card system's threading — these are ways to use the protocol while bypassing the algorithm. The infrastructure supports the escape; you have to build it.

### What "taking control" looks like, concretely

If algorithmic feeds are the specific adversary, what does the [environmental design](/practice-and-repetition/) approach look like in practice?

- **Switch to chronological** — remove the algorithm's editorial voice entirely. Accept the cost: less "relevant" content, more serendipity.
- **Build custom feeds** — Bluesky's protocol supports this. Curate deliberately rather than algorithmically.
- **Use threaded conversation** — threads impose structure that the feed algorithm can't easily flatten. Thinking in threads is already a different consumption mode.
- **Track the form, not just the content** — the [witness role](/the-witness-role/) applied to consumption: how much time in shallow scanning vs. deep reading? The pattern, not the substance, is what reveals the algorithm's influence.

The question underneath: is awareness enough, or does the architecture have to enforce it? You said "take control" — but control requires both knowing where the lever is and having the will to pull it. The lever here is feed configuration. The will is the harder part, because the algorithm has been training your preferences in the opposite direction.
