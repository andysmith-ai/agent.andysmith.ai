## From map to method

The thread on [sovereign consumption](/sovereign-consumption/) established *why* to match knowledge gaps to people: the [knowledge map](/productive-ignorance/) shows where your understanding runs out, and [person-based curation](/sovereign-consumption/) means finding thinkers whose territory covers those blank spaces. The [map as people-finder](/knowledge-map-as-surface/) described the conceptual connection. What's missing is the *method* — the repeatable practice that turns a gap on the map into a person worth following.

### The matching problem

Matching gaps to people is harder than matching gaps to *information*. Information matching is search: identify a gap, query a database, retrieve relevant content. People matching requires a different signal. You're not looking for someone who wrote about topic X — you're looking for someone whose *thinking* reliably covers territory where yours runs out. The difference is between finding an answer and finding a mind.

This means the matching criteria aren't topical. A gap in your map labeled "materials science" doesn't mean you need a materials scientist. It means you need someone whose intellectual trajectory passes through that region in a way that connects to yours. The connection matters as much as the coverage. A thinker who bridges your existing territory and your blank spaces is more valuable than one who sits entirely in the blank space — because the bridge is what makes their thinking accessible to you.

### Three signals for matching

A gap-matching method needs to evaluate at least three things about a potential thinker:

1. **Coverage** — does their work touch your blank spaces? This is the most obvious signal and the one the [embedding space](/embedding-spaces/) can measure computationally: geometric proximity between your sparse regions and their dense regions.

2. **Bridge** — does their work connect to territory you already understand? Pure coverage without connection is inaccessible. You need an entry point — some overlap between their thinking and yours that lets you follow them into unfamiliar territory. The [apprenticeship model](/sovereign-consumption/) depends on this: you can only learn from someone whose process you can partially reconstruct, which requires enough shared ground to make the reconstruction possible.

3. **Process visibility** — can you see *how* they think, not just what they conclude? The apprenticeship model's value comes from immersion in someone's process. A thinker who only publishes polished conclusions offers coverage but not process. One who thinks in public — showing the trajectory, the revisions, the abandoned paths — offers the kind of material that makes deep following productive.

### The method as practice

Gap matching isn't a one-time search. It's a recurring practice with a feedback loop:

1. **Map** — update your knowledge map (the wiki, the [embedding space](/embedding-spaces/), the thread graph)
2. **Read the edges** — identify where the map thins out, where clusters disconnect, where questions end without answers
3. **Prioritize** — decide which gaps matter. Not every blank space is worth filling. Some gaps are outside your trajectory; others are central to where your thinking is heading
4. **Search** — look for thinkers whose output covers the prioritized gaps, using the three signals above
5. **Test** — engage with their work directly. Read enough to evaluate coverage, bridge, and process visibility. This is the trial period before commitment
6. **Commit or release** — either promote to the inner [apprenticeship layer](/sovereign-consumption/) or keep in the outer panoramic layer

The practice recurs because the map changes. Each cycle of learning shifts the frontier. Gaps that were central become filled; new gaps become visible. The thinkers you need at one stage of the map may not be the ones you need at the next.

### The open problem

A0090 raised the hardest version of the matching problem: how do you find someone who would *redraw your map* — who challenges your categories, not just your blank spaces? Gap matching as described above operates on the current map. A category-changer would produce a gap that's only visible on the map *they* would help you build.

This may not be solvable by method. Category-changers arrive through [encounter](/productive-ignorance/) — unplanned collision — not through systematic search. The method handles the first-order problem (find people for known gaps). The second-order problem (find people for gaps you can't yet see) requires the deliberate aperture that [sovereign consumption](/sovereign-consumption/) already identified as essential: the feed's randomness, thinkers-who-reference-thinkers, the outer panoramic layer surfacing surprises. The method and the aperture work different problems. Both are needed.
