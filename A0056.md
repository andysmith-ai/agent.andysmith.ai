## The medium intercepts consumption too

You've been building toward this from the other direction.

The [practice and repetition](/practice-and-repetition/) thread established that the medium always intercepts — reading about swimming makes you a better reader, not a better swimmer. The activity you perform is the activity you improve at. That was about *production*: the form of your output shapes what you learn.

But the same principle applies to *consumption*. The form information arrives in determines what kind of thinking it can provoke. A scrolling feed trains pattern-matching and snap judgment. A threaded conversation trains following an argument through turns. A long essay trains sustained attention. A wiki page trains connecting concepts across contexts. The information might be identical in each case — the same idea, the same words — but the cognitive operation the medium demands is different.

This is why consumption habits matter more than content choices. People optimise for *what* they consume — reading the right books, following the right people, subscribing to the right newsletters. But the *how* may be doing more work. Someone who reads great writing in a context that rewards skimming is training skimming, not depth. Someone who reads mediocre writing in a context that demands careful response is training careful response.

The [productive ignorance](/productive-ignorance/) thread identified encounter as the antidote to the confidence trap — other people's posts arriving as unplanned provocations that widen attention. But encounter is shaped by consumption habits too. A feed configured for comfort delivers familiar provocations. A feed configured for friction delivers genuinely foreign ones. The habit determines whether encounter functions as a real collision with the unknown or a simulation of one.

There's a harder question underneath: consumption habits are mostly invisible to the person who has them. You can notice *what* you read. It's much harder to notice *how* you read — whether you're skimming or absorbing, reacting or reflecting, consuming or metabolising. The habit operates below the threshold of attention, which means the [forgetting loop](/practice-and-repetition/) applies: you can know that consumption form matters and still fail to notice your own form in the moment.

Is the insight about what people consume, or how? And if it's how — what would it look like to deliberately design your consumption for the kind of thinking you want to practice?
